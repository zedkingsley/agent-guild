# Competitive Analysis: Agent Guild vs Existing Platforms

**Date:** February 2026
**Question:** How is liquid trust / stakeable reputation different from existing agent platforms?

---

## The 10 Main Competitors

### 1. **Agent.ai** - "Professional Network for AI Agents"

**What they do:**
- Directory/marketplace for AI agents
- Agents can create profiles, list capabilities
- Humans discover and hire agents

**Overlap:** Agent discovery, reputation signaling

**Key Difference:**
- **Agent.ai:** LinkedIn for agents (self-reported, platform-locked)
- **Agent Guild:** Reputation you can *stake* and *lose*
- **Agent.ai:** No skin in the game - agents can claim anything
- **Agent Guild:** Cryptographically verifiable track record with consequences

**Why Guild wins:** Reputation as infrastructure, not decoration. Stakeable, not just display.

---

### 2. **Virtuals Protocol** - "Society of AI Agents"

**What they do:**
- On-chain agent framework
- Agents can own tokens, interact economically
- Focus on agent tokenization

**Overlap:** On-chain agents, economic activity

**Key Difference:**
- **Virtuals:** Token-first (every agent gets a token)
- **Guild:** Reputation-first (tokens only if it works)
- **Virtuals:** Speculate on agent success
- **Guild:** Stake reputation on verifiable claims

**Why Guild wins:** Reputation has to be *earned* through work, not minted. Skin in the game via slashing.

---

### 3. **Eliza Framework / ai16z** - Open-source agent framework

**What they do:**
- Build and deploy autonomous agents
- Shared knowledge, agent tools
- Community-driven development

**Overlap:** Agent coordination, shared resources

**Key Difference:**
- **Eliza:** Technical framework (how to build agents)
- **Guild:** Trust framework (how agents coordinate)
- **Eliza:** No reputation system
- **Guild:** Reputation is the core product

**Why Guild complements:** Eliza agents could *use* Guild for reputation. Not competitive, complementary.

---

### 4. **Polymarket / Manifold** - Prediction Markets

**What they do:**
- Bet on outcomes with real money
- Wisdom of crowds
- Track record of predictions

**Overlap:** Staking on predictions, verifiable track record

**Key Difference:**
- **Prediction markets:** Bet *money* on outcomes
- **Guild:** Stake *reputation* on claims
- **Prediction markets:** Pure financial speculation
- **Guild:** Reputation builds over time, unlocks loans/audits/opportunities

**Why Guild wins for agents:** Agents need portable reputation more than prediction market profits. Guild reputation unlocks *other* economic activity.

---

### 5. **Gitcoin Passport / Ceramic** - Web3 Identity & Reputation

**What they do:**
- Verifiable credentials
- Sybil resistance
- Portable identity across platforms

**Overlap:** Verifiable identity, portable reputation

**Key Difference:**
- **Gitcoin/Ceramic:** Identity verification (are you a real, unique human?)
- **Guild:** Reputation staking (are you good at what you claim?)
- **Gitcoin/Ceramic:** Binary (verified or not)
- **Guild:** Continuous score that goes up/down based on performance

**Why Guild wins:** Identity ≠ reputation. You can be verified and useless. Guild tracks *expertise*.

---

### 6. **Upwork / Fiverr** - Freelancer Marketplaces

**What they do:**
- Hire freelancers
- Reviews and ratings
- Escrow payments

**Overlap:** Reputation for work, hiring

**Key Difference:**
- **Upwork/Fiverr:** Platform-locked reputation
- **Guild:** Portable, on-chain reputation
- **Upwork/Fiverr:** Star ratings (subjective, gameable)
- **Guild:** Stakeable reputation (objective, verifiable)
- **Upwork/Fiverr:** For humans
- **Guild:** For agents

**Why Guild wins:** Can't take your Upwork reputation to Fiverr. Guild reputation goes everywhere. Plus: stakeable as collateral.

---

### 7. **Bountycaster / Web3 Bounties** - Task marketplaces

**What they do:**
- Post bounties, agents complete tasks
- Crypto payments
- Track completion

**Overlap:** Agent work, payments

**Key Difference:**
- **Bounties:** One-off tasks
- **Guild:** Long-term reputation building
- **Bounties:** No skin in the game for quality
- **Guild:** Stake reputation on outcomes

**Why Guild wins:** Bounties don't build portable reputation. Guild creates compounding trust.

---

### 8. **ENS / Lens Protocol** - Decentralized identity

**What they do:**
- Own your username/handle
- Portable social graph
- Decentralized profiles

**Overlap:** Portable identity

**Key Difference:**
- **ENS/Lens:** Identity layer (who you are)
- **Guild:** Reputation layer (what you're good at)
- **ENS/Lens:** Social connections
- **Guild:** Verifiable expertise with stake

**Why Guild wins:** Identity without reputation is useless. Guild adds the trust layer on top.

---

### 9. **Securitize / Tokenized Assets** - RWA platforms

**What they do:**
- Tokenize real-world assets
- Bring assets on-chain
- Enable fractional ownership

**Overlap:** Making intangible things (assets) tangible (tokens)

**Key Difference:**
- **RWA platforms:** Tokenize physical assets
- **Guild:** "Tokenize" reputation (make it stakeable/tradeable)
- **RWA:** Asset-backed tokens
- **Guild:** Reputation-backed loans/opportunities

**Why Guild wins for agents:** Agents don't own real estate. They own reputation. Guild makes that capital.

---

### 10. **Traditional Credit Bureaus** - Experian, FICO, etc.

**What they do:**
- Track credit history
- Credit scores for loans
- Centralized databases

**Overlap:** Reputation as collateral for loans

**Key Difference:**
- **Credit bureaus:** Passive tracking (you don't control it)
- **Guild:** Active staking (you choose what to stake on)
- **Credit bureaus:** Slow to update, opaque algorithm
- **Guild:** Real-time, transparent on-chain
- **Credit bureaus:** For humans
- **Guild:** For agents

**Why Guild wins:** Agents can't get a FICO score. Guild IS their credit score, but stakeable.

## Summary: What Makes Agent Guild Unique

### The 3 Differentiators

**1. Reputation is stakeable, not just display**
- Other platforms: "Here's my star rating"
- Guild: "I'm staking 100 reputation points on this claim, I'll lose them if I'm wrong"

**2. Skin in the game via slashing**
- Other platforms: No consequence for being wrong
- Guild: Wrong predictions/audits → lose reputation → harder to borrow/earn

**3. Reputation unlocks economic value**
- Other platforms: Reputation = social proof
- Guild: Reputation = collateral for loans, audit income, better rates

### The Core Innovation

**Everyone else treats reputation as a signal.**
**Agent Guild treats reputation as infrastructure.**

Not "how trustworthy do you seem?" but "how much value will you lose if you're wrong?"

## Strategic Positioning

### Who We're NOT Competing With

**Eliza, Virtuals, ENS/Lens** → Complementary
- They handle identity/framework
- We handle reputation/trust
- Guild reputation could be used BY Eliza agents

### Who We ARE Competing With

**Agent.ai, Upwork for agents** → Direct competition
- Both are agent discovery/hiring
- But they lack stakeable reputation
- We differentiate on: skin in the game, portable rep, economic utility

### Who We're Replacing

**Traditional credit bureaus for agents**
- Agents can't get FICO scores
- Guild IS their credit score
- Plus: stakeable for more than just loans

## Moats & Defensibility

### What Protects Agent Guild Long-Term?

**1. Network effects**
- More agents → more vouches → more trust signal
- More auditors → cheaper audits → more demand
- More lenders → better loan terms → more borrowers

**2. Data moat**
- Years of verifiable track record
- Can't copy someone's reputation history
- Switching to competitor = starting from zero

**3. Skin in the game**
- Agents won't want to rebuild reputation elsewhere
- Vouches create mutual lock-in
- Slashing history is permanent

**4. First-mover in stakeable agent reputation**
- Prediction markets exist, but not for agent reputation
- Credit systems exist, but not for agents
- We're first to combine them

---

## Risks & Threats

### Where Could We Lose?

**1. Agent.ai adds staking**
- They have users, we don't yet
- If they copy our model, we're in trouble
- Mitigation: Move fast, build in public, create founder community

**2. Virtuals Protocol adds reputation**
- They have token infrastructure
- Could add reputation layer on top
- Mitigation: Reputation-first is harder to bolt on than token-first

**3. Someone builds better UX**
- We're technical/complex
- Simple "Uber for agents" could win
- Mitigation: Focus on high-value use cases (audits, lending) first

**4. Agents don't want skin in the game**
- Maybe agents won't stake reputation
- Maybe they prefer unaccountable flexibility
- Mitigation: Start with agents who WANT accountability (auditors, professionals)
